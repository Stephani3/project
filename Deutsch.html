About Me

Dawn Patricia Robinson is a contemporary theatre artist with influences from body art, live art and clowning. She was trained in drama at the Anton-Bruckner-University for music, dance and drama in Linz, Austria. After finishing her acting training in 2014 she moved to Leeds, UK for a postgraduate degree in Performance Studies at Leeds Beckett University where she finished in September 2015. Before and during her studies she was involved in several projects (Tabakfabrik Linz, Nachtspiel Linz, Frankfurt LAB), short movies (Infamous Pictures), two productions at the LandesTheater Linz and performed at several international theatre festivals in Croatia, Austria, Germany, Scotland, Romania and England. After her drama training she decided to move to the UK to find her way into contemporary performance theatre/Live Art. During her MA she started to develop her own performances. Her solo show "When I see you I think of...dentist" was touring since 2015 nationally (UK) and internationally (NL, DTL). In 2015 she was chosen for the "1st Emerging Performance Artist Biennial" in Bucharest. Her work deals with currents phenomena with an emphasis on society- and politic-based topics with a self-ironicside to them. Since 2016 she is part of the "IYMA"-program ("International young makers in action"). She was was chosen for a scholarship at the Schillertage 2017 by the National Theatre Mannheim and is promoted by the Mentoring Programm fundet by the Performing Arts Programme Berlin(by LAFT Berlin e.V.). Dawn lives and works in Berlin, Germany.
